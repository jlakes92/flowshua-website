The operational problem

Experienced technicians often carry years of practical knowledge that is difficult to transfer consistently. New employees may learn through informal handoffs, repeat questions, and trial and error. This concept is designed to help teams capture recurring discrepancies, document proven escalation paths, and turn completed service calls into a more structured learning resource.

How the system works

Customer-reported issue → technician observations → likely causes ranked by company experience → approved verification process → escalation when needed → final resolution → lessons learned → updated training pathway.

What the workbook includes

  • A discrepancy log for recurring service issues
  • Common discrepancy pathways with cause ranking, escalation points, evidence requirements, and coaching focus
  • A dashboard for recurring issue categories, repeat-call risk, escalations, and training readiness
  • Editable dropdown lists that can be adapted for HVAC, plumbing, electrical, facilities, propane, or other trade-service teams

Example workflow

01Log the customer-reported issue and the conditions observed in the field.
02Connect the issue to common discrepancy pathways and likely causes based on company experience.
03Document required evidence, approved verification steps, and the point where escalation is needed.
04Record the final resolution and convert useful lessons into coaching notes for future onboarding.

Potential business value

Designed to support more consistent onboarding, preserve senior technician knowledge, improve documentation quality, identify recurring issues, and help leaders focus training where it is needed most.

Important boundaries

This portfolio project uses illustrative HVAC service data to demonstrate a field knowledge-management workflow. It is not a repair manual or a substitute for qualified technical judgment, manufacturer documentation, applicable codes, licensing requirements, or company safety procedures.
